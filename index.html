What is a Megger?

Also known as an Insulation Resistance Tester, the Megger is a specialized ohmmeter used to measure the electrical resistance of insulators.

Principle

It works on the principle of electromagnetic induction. It generates a high DC voltage (500V, 1000V) to measure very high resistance values in Mega-Ohms (MΩ).

Why Use It?

To detect insulation breakdown which might not show up at low voltages but can cause catastrophic failure at operating voltages.

Components

Key parts include a DC generator (hand-cranked or battery), a Current Coil, a Pressure (Voltage) Coil, a deflecting needle, and connection terminals.

Working Principle

The Megger operates as a Moving Coil Instrument. The deflection of the pointer depends on the ratio of the currents in the two coils (Coil A & Coil B). This ratio is proportional to the voltage divided by the current (V/I), which equals Resistance (R).
